Russian Mathematical Olympiad 2012

Interesting Problem · English

Problem. Initially, there are \(111\) equal-weight pieces of clay on the table. In one operation, we may choose some or all of the pieces, divide them into several groups with the same number of pieces in each group, and then merge each group into one piece. Suppose that after \(m\) operations there are exactly \(11\) pieces on the table, and their weights are pairwise different. Find the minimum possible value of \(m\).

Answer. \(\boxed{2}\).

Proof

One operation is not enough. Since all pieces initially have the same weight, after one operation all newly formed pieces have the same weight, and all untouched pieces still have the original weight. Hence after one operation there can be at most two different weights, so it is impossible to obtain \(11\) pieces with pairwise different weights. Therefore \(m\ge 2\).

Now we show that two operations are enough. In the first operation, choose \(74\) pieces and divide them into \(37\) groups of \(2\) pieces each. After merging, we get \(37\) pieces of weight \(2\), while \(37\) pieces of weight \(1\) remain.

In the second operation, leave one piece of weight \(1\) and one piece of weight \(2\) untouched. There remain \(36\) pieces of weight \(1\) and \(36\) pieces of weight \(2\). Divide them into \(9\) groups of \(8\) pieces each: for \(i=0,1,\dots,8\), let the \(i\)-th group contain \(i\) pieces of weight \(2\) and \(8-i\) pieces of weight \(1\). This uses exactly

\[ 0+1+\cdots+8=36 \]

pieces of weight \(2\), and exactly

\[ 8+7+\cdots+0=36 \]

pieces of weight \(1\). After merging these groups, we obtain \(9\) pieces with weights

\[ 8,9,10,11,12,13,14,15,16. \]

Together with the two untouched pieces of weights \(1\) and \(2\), the final \(11\) pieces have weights

\[ 1,2,8,9,10,11,12,13,14,15,16, \]

which are pairwise different. Thus \(m\le 2\). Since \(m\ge 2\), the minimum value is

\[ \boxed{2}. \]
